Answer the following about the response of the bacteria to the bloom:

  1. Using data shown in the second figure, estimate the bacterial yield in terms of maximum increase in carbon biomass (gC per liter) of the heterotrophic bacteria during the bloom. Use a value of 15 fgC (10-15 gC) per bacterial cell for your calculation. Dont forget that the bacterial abundances in the figure are in cells per ml, and you are calculating biomass as gC per liter. Show your calculations.
  2. Estimate an amount of total bacterial carbon utilization based on the biomass yield you calculated. Use a bacterial growth efficiency of 25 % for your calculation.

Explain what the magnitude of bacterial utilization of organic carbon implies about total phytoplankton production (both Particulate and dissolved organic carbon; POC and DOC, respectively) during the bloom, including the POC production which resulted in accumulation of phytoplankton biomass.
